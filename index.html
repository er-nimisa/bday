My Girl, Nimisa

A little love story written just for you – a bit late on the calendar, but always on time in my heart.

1

Once upon a class, there was us

Somewhere in a normal school day, there was a Web Dev training class. On one side of the room were the big brothers and sisters from class 12, ready to teach. On the other side were the juniors from class 10, eyes full of curiosity and a little bit of nervousness.

In that room, between HTML tags and CSS colors, there was a girl named Nimisa. She listened carefully, tried hard, smiled softly, and without even knowing it, she turned that simple training into something unforgettable.

At first, they called each other brother and sister – not by blood, but by bond. She didn’t know it yet, but in someone’s heart, that classroom was no longer just a class. It was the place where a story quietly began.

2

The “teacher” who noticed everything

There was a boy in that Web Dev batch, standing on the “teacher” side, pretending to be calm and serious. But inside, every time he saw Nimisa focus on the screen or ask a doubt, his heart quietly smiled like a shy sun behind the clouds.

He noticed the small things: the way she tried to understand every concept, the way she didn’t give up easily, the way her presence made the class feel lighter, like a soft song playing in the background while everyone else only heard noise.

Day by day, between “brother–sister” jokes and shared moments, something gentle began to grow. Slowly, quietly, he realised the universe had hidden a gift in that classroom. Not in the computers, not in the projector, but sitting quietly in the class 10 row. And that gift was her.

3

Bhadra 17 (or 18): when he chose her

Time kept moving. Brother–sister by bond slowly turned into something deeper – a feeling that didn’t fit in just one word. One day around Bhadra 17 or 18, his heart finally spoke the truth it had been holding for so long.

On that day, he looked at his girl from class 10 and chose her not just as a student, not just as a sister by bond, but as the one his heart wanted to walk beside. He didn’t just say words – he proposed his feelings and trusted the sky with the answer.

Now, as she moves toward her SEE, books open and nights getting longer, one thing stays the same: he believes in her the way the moon believes in the night sky – calmly, fully, without any doubt. No exam, no mark, no result can change how proud he already is of her.

4

Things he loves about his Nimisa

  • The way her name, Nimisa, feels like a warm hug every time he says it, whether in class or in his heart.
  • Her courage to learn, even when topics feel confusing or heavy.
  • The way she listens, asks, and tries again, turning doubt into understanding.
  • Her soft heart that still finds kindness for others, even while she is stressed herself.
  • The way she makes him want to be better – as a person, as a “teacher”, as someone who stands beside her.
  • And most of all, the simple fact that she is her – beautifully, wonderfully, irreplaceably her.
5

For your birthday and your dreams

Dear Nimisa, if I could wrap love in pages, it would look like this – simple, honest, and full of you.

I wish your days ahead are full of good grades, happy walks home, silly laughs with friends, peaceful nights of sleep, and a heart that always feels proud of itself.

On your birthday and every day after, remember this: you are loved, more than these words, more than this screen, more than this little website can ever show.

Happy Birthday, my beautiful girl, and all the best for your SEE. I am always cheering for you, always proud of you, always here for you – in every exam, every result, every chapter of your life. ♡
